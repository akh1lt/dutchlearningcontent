# Dutch A2 Sentence Taxonomy
## A Practical Pattern-Based Classification System

**Purpose**: Tag 1,265 Dutch sentences to identify ~100 high-ROI memorizable patterns for A2-level learners preparing for inburgering exams and daily communication.

**Core Philosophy**: This taxonomy prioritizes PRACTICAL COMMUNICATION PATTERNS over exhaustive grammatical coverage. Tags should help learners recognize "I've seen this pattern before" and practice strategically.

---

## 1. COMMUNICATIVE INTENT (Primary Category)
*What is the speaker trying to DO with this sentence?*

### 1.1 Requesting & Asking
**Learning Value**: These are the bread-and-butter patterns for daily survival
- Direct requests: "Kun je mij helpen?" / "Mag ik...?"
- Polite requests: "Zou je...?" / "Wil je alsjeblieft...?"
- Permission seeking: "Mag ik...?" / "Is het goed als...?"
- Offers of help: "Zal ik...?" / "Kan ik iets voor je doen?"
- **Exam Focus**: A2 speaking tasks constantly require polite requesting

### 1.2 Information Seeking (Questions)
**Learning Value**: High-frequency patterns for getting answers
- Yes/No questions: "Heb je...?" / "Is er...?" / "Kun je...?"
- Wh-questions: "Waar is...?" / "Wanneer...?" / "Hoe...?" / "Wat voor...?"
- Indirect questions: "Weet je waar...?" / "Kun je me zeggen hoe...?"
- Clarification: "Bedoel je...?" / "Wat betekent...?"
- **Exam Focus**: Essential for KNM exam listening/speaking sections

### 1.3 Stating & Informing
**Learning Value**: Foundational declarative patterns
- Simple statements: "Ik woon in..." / "Dat is..."
- Describing situations: "Er is/zijn..." / "Het gaat om..."
- Giving information: "Het station is..." / "De winkel sluit om..."
- Reporting facts: "Ik heb gehoord dat..." / "Het is belangrijk dat..."
- **Exam Focus**: Reading comprehension and written communication

### 1.4 Expressing Needs & Wants
**Learning Value**: Critical for service interactions
- Direct needs: "Ik heb ... nodig" / "Ik moet..."
- Preferences: "Ik wil graag..." / "Ik zou liever..."
- Wishes: "Ik hoop dat..." / "Ik zou graag willen..."
- Problems: "Ik heb een probleem met..." / "Het lukt me niet om..."
- **Exam Focus**: Healthcare, housing, workplace scenarios

### 1.5 Reacting & Responding
**Learning Value**: Natural conversation flow
- Agreement: "Ja, dat klopt" / "Ik ben het met je eens"
- Disagreement: "Nee, dat is niet zo" / "Ik denk het niet"
- Expressing surprise: "Echt waar?" / "Dat wist ik niet"
- Confirming understanding: "Oké, ik begrijp het" / "Dus je bedoelt..."
- **Exam Focus**: Speaking fluency and natural interaction

### 1.6 Suggesting & Advising
**Learning Value**: Social interaction patterns
- Suggestions: "Zullen we...?" / "Wat dacht je van...?" / "Misschien kunnen we..."
- Advice: "Je moet..." / "Het is beter om..." / "Je kunt het beste..."
- Recommendations: "Ik raad je aan om..." / "Je zou moeten..."
- **Exam Focus**: Social integration scenarios

### 1.7 Explaining & Reasoning
**Learning Value**: Connecting ideas logically
- Giving reasons: "omdat..." / "want..." / "daarom..."
- Purpose: "om ... te" / "zodat..."
- Conditions: "als..." / "als je ... dan..."
- Contrast: "maar..." / "hoewel..." / "toch..."
- **Exam Focus**: Extended responses in speaking exam

### 1.8 Commands & Instructions
**Learning Value**: Following/giving directions
- Direct imperatives: "Ga naar..." / "Neem..."
- Softened commands: "Doe ... maar" / "Kom even hier"
- Instructions: "Je moet eerst... en dan..." / "Let op dat..."
- Prohibitions: "Niet ... !" / "Je mag niet..."
- **Exam Focus**: Following directions, workplace safety

## 2. VERB PATTERNS (Grammar Core)
*What structural pattern does the verb create?*

### 2.1 Single Finite Verb (Simple Present/Past)
**Learning Value**: Foundation patterns
- Present: "Ik werk hier" / "Hij woont daar"
- Simple past: "Ik werkte" / "Hij was" / "Ze hadden"
- **Pattern Recognition**: Subject + finite verb + rest
- **Frequency**: 40-50% of daily sentences

### 2.2 Perfect Tense (Hebben/Zijn + Participle)
**Learning Value**: Past experiences, completed actions
- With hebben: "Ik heb gewerkt" / "Ze heeft gebeld"
- With zijn: "Ik ben gegaan" / "Ze zijn geweest"
- **Pattern Recognition**: Auxiliary position + participle at end
- **Exam Focus**: Describing past events in speaking

### 2.3 Modal Verb Constructions
**Learning Value**: Expressing ability, permission, obligation
- Kunnen: "Ik kan zwemmen" / "Kun je dat doen?"
- Mogen: "Mag ik...?" / "Je mag niet..."
- Moeten: "Ik moet werken" / "Je moet dat doen"
- Willen: "Ik wil graag..." / "Wil je...?"
- Zullen: "Zullen we...?" / "Ik zal het doen"
- **Pattern Recognition**: Modal + infinitive at end
- **Frequency**: Top 10 most common patterns

### 2.4 Gaan + Infinitive (Near Future)
**Learning Value**: Plans and intentions
- Future plans: "Ik ga morgen..." / "We gaan ...en"
- Intentions: "Ik ga proberen om..."
- **Pattern Recognition**: Gaan conjugated + infinitive at end
- **Exam Focus**: Discussing plans in speaking

### 2.5 Te + Infinitive (Purpose/Obligation)
**Learning Value**: Expressing goals and requirements
- Om te: "Ik ben hier om te leren" / "Het is moeilijk om te..."
- Te + infinitive: "Ik heb geen tijd te verliezen"
- Adjective + te: "Het is belangrijk te weten"
- **Pattern Recognition**: Watch for "om te" vs. bare "te"

### 2.6 Progressive (Aan het + Infinitive)
**Learning Value**: Ongoing actions
- "Ik ben aan het werken" / "Ze zijn aan het eten"
- **Pattern Recognition**: Zijn + aan het + infinitive
- **Note**: Less frequent than English progressive, but distinctive

### 2.7 Separable Verbs
**Learning Value**: Common in everyday speech
- Main clause: "Ik bel je straks op" / "Kom je mee?"
- Subordinate: "...omdat ik je opbel"
- With modal: "Ik wil je opbellen"
- **Pattern Recognition**: Particle position changes
- **High-Frequency Examples**: meegaan, opbellen, aankomen, uitgaan

## 3. WORD ORDER PATTERNS (Structural)
*How is the sentence structured?*

### 3.1 Standard Main Clause (V2)
**Learning Value**: Foundation structure
- Subject first: "Ik werk morgen" / "De winkel is open"
- Time/place first: "Morgen werk ik" / "Hier is het station"
- **Pattern Recognition**: Verb always in second position
- **Exam Focus**: Production accuracy matters

### 3.2 Subordinate Clause (Verb-Final)
**Learning Value**: Complex sentence building
- Omdat: "...omdat ik ziek ben"
- Dat: "Ik weet dat hij morgen komt"
- Als: "Als je tijd hebt, ..."
- **Pattern Recognition**: All verbs cluster at end
- **Common Error Point**: Learners forget verb-final order

### 3.3 Question Word Order
**Learning Value**: Information gathering
- Yes/No: "Heb je...?" / "Is er...?" (verb-first)
- Wh-questions: "Waar woon je?" / "Wanneer kom je?" (wh + verb + subject)
- **Pattern Recognition**: Inversion after question word

### 3.4 Existential ER Constructions
**Learning Value**: Presenting new information
- "Er is..." / "Er zijn..." / "Er zijn veel mensen"
- "Er komt..." / "Er staat..." / "Er ligt..."
- **Pattern Recognition**: Sentence-initial "er" + verb
- **Exam Focus**: Descriptions in speaking/writing

## 4. TIME FRAME (Temporal Context)
*When does this happen?*

### 4.1 Present Situations
**Learning Value**: Current state descriptions
- Habitual: "Ik werk elke dag"
- Right now: "Ik ben nu thuis"
- General facts: "Nederland is klein"

### 4.2 Past Events
**Learning Value**: Storytelling and experience sharing
- Recent past: "Ik heb gisteren..." / "Vanmorgen ben ik..."
- Distant past: "Vroeger woonde ik..." / "Toen was ik..."
- **Exam Focus**: Biographical information

### 4.3 Future Plans
**Learning Value**: Scheduling and planning
- Near future: "Ik ga morgen..." / "Straks kom ik..."
- Future intentions: "Ik zal..." / "Volgend jaar ga ik..."
- **Exam Focus**: Making appointments, discussing plans

### 4.4 Time Connectors
**Learning Value**: Sequencing events
- Sequence: "eerst... dan... daarna"
- Duration: "sinds..." / "al..." / "tot..."
- Frequency: "altijd" / "vaak" / "soms" / "nooit"

---

## 5. MODAL & ATTITUDE MARKERS
*What's the speaker's stance or certainty level?*

### 5.1 Certainty & Possibility
**Learning Value**: Hedging and confidence
- Certain: "Ik weet zeker dat..." / "Het is duidelijk dat..."
- Possible: "Misschien..." / "Waarschijnlijk..." / "Het kan zijn dat..."
- Uncertain: "Ik weet niet of..." / "Ik denk dat..."

### 5.2 Necessity & Obligation
**Learning Value**: Rules and requirements
- Must: "Je moet..." / "Het is nodig om..."
- Should: "Je zou moeten..." / "Het is belangrijk dat..."
- Not necessary: "Je hoeft niet..." / "Het is niet nodig"

### 5.3 Politeness & Softening
**Learning Value**: Social appropriateness
- Modal particles: "maar" / "even" / "eens" / "hoor"
- Conditional: "Zou je...?" / "Ik zou graag..."
- Hedging: "eigenlijk" / "een beetje" / "misschien"
- **Exam Focus**: Natural-sounding speech

### 5.4 Contrast & Emphasis
**Learning Value**: Nuanced communication
- Contrast: "wel" / "toch" / "juist"
- Emphasis: "echt" / "heel" / "zo"
- Correction: "niet ... maar ..." / "eigenlijk"

## 6. NEGATION PATTERNS
*How is negation expressed?*

### 6.1 Verb Negation (niet)
**Learning Value**: Denying actions
- Standard: "Ik werk niet" / "Hij komt niet"
- With object: "Ik zie hem niet" / "Ik begrijp het niet"
- **Pattern Recognition**: Position matters (usually sentence-final)

### 6.2 Noun Negation (geen)
**Learning Value**: Absence of things
- "Ik heb geen tijd" / "Er is geen probleem"
- "Geen mensen" / "Geen geld"
- **Pattern Recognition**: Geen replaces "een" or plural

### 6.3 Never/Nothing/Nobody
**Learning Value**: Absolute negation
- Nooit: "Ik ga nooit..." / "Hij komt nooit"
- Niets: "Ik weet niets" / "Niets is belangrijk"
- Niemand: "Niemand is hier" / "Ik zie niemand"

---

## 7. CONNECTING & COMBINING
*How are ideas linked together?*

### 7.1 Coordination (Main + Main)
**Learning Value**: Simple combining
- Addition: "en" / "ook" / "bovendien"
- Contrast: "maar" / "toch"
- Choice: "of"
- Cause: "want" / "dus"
- **Pattern Recognition**: Both clauses keep main clause order

### 7.2 Subordination (Main + Subordinate)
**Learning Value**: Complex reasoning
- Reason: "omdat" / "doordat"
- Condition: "als" / "wanneer"
- Time: "toen" / "nadat" / "voordat"
- Contrast: "hoewel" / "terwijl"
- **Pattern Recognition**: Subordinate clause = verb-final

### 7.3 Relative Clauses
**Learning Value**: Describing people/things
- Die: "De man die..." (de-word)
- Dat: "Het huis dat..." (het-word)
- Waar: "De stad waar ik woon"
- **Pattern Recognition**: Verb-final in relative clause

## 8. PRACTICAL SITUATION CLUSTERS
*Real-world context categories*

### 8.1 Healthcare & Body
**Exam Relevance**: HIGH (KNM scenario topic)
- Appointments: "Ik heb een afspraak" / "Kan ik een afspraak maken?"
- Symptoms: "Ik heb pijn" / "Ik voel me niet goed"
- Instructions: "Neem deze medicijnen" / "Rust goed uit"

### 8.2 Housing & Living
**Exam Relevance**: HIGH (KNM scenario topic)
- Finding housing: "Ik zoek een huis" / "Hoeveel kost de huur?"
- Problems: "De verwarming is kapot" / "Er is een lek"
- Describing: "Het appartement heeft..." / "De keuken is..."

### 8.3 Work & Employment
**Exam Relevance**: HIGH (KNM scenario topic)
- Job seeking: "Ik zoek werk" / "Waar kan ik solliciteren?"
- Work situations: "Ik werk bij..." / "Mijn werkgever is..."
- Problems: "Ik ben ziek" / "Ik kan niet komen"

### 8.4 Shopping & Services
**Exam Relevance**: MEDIUM-HIGH (daily communication)
- Asking prices: "Hoeveel kost...?" / "Wat is de prijs?"
- Making purchases: "Ik wil graag..." / "Mag ik..."
- Complaints: "Dit werkt niet" / "Ik wil dit ruilen"

### 8.5 Transportation & Directions
**Exam Relevance**: MEDIUM-HIGH (daily communication)
- Asking directions: "Waar is...?" / "Hoe kom ik bij...?"
- Public transport: "Welke bus moet ik nemen?" / "Waar moet ik uitstappen?"
- Travel: "Ik ga naar..." / "Ik kom uit..."

### 8.6 Social & Family
**Exam Relevance**: MEDIUM (speaking exam topics)
- Introductions: "Dit is..." / "Mag ik je voorstellen?"
- Family: "Ik heb ... kinderen" / "Mijn partner is..."
- Plans: "Zullen we...?" / "Heb je zin om...?"

### 8.7 Education & Learning
**Exam Relevance**: MEDIUM (common learner context)
- School situations: "Ik ga naar school" / "Ik volg een cursus"
- Understanding: "Ik begrijp het niet" / "Kun je dat uitleggen?"
- Progress: "Ik leer Nederlands" / "Het gaat goed"

### 8.8 Money & Administration
**Exam Relevance**: HIGH (practical necessity)
- Banking: "Ik wil geld opnemen" / "Waar is de bank?"
- Bills: "Ik moet betalen" / "Hoeveel moet ik betalen?"
- Documents: "Ik heb een ... nodig" / "Waar kan ik een ... krijgen?"

## 9. PRONOUNS & REFERENCE
*Who/what is being talked about?*

### 9.1 Personal Pronouns
**Learning Value**: Subject vs. object forms
- Subject: ik / jij / hij / zij / wij / jullie / ze
- Object: mij / jou / hem / haar / ons / jullie / hen/ze
- **Common Errors**: je/jij confusion, hen/hun distinction

### 9.2 Possessive Forms
**Learning Value**: Ownership and relationships
- Mijn / jouw / zijn / haar / ons/onze / jullie / hun
- **Pattern Recognition**: onze (de-word) vs. ons (het-word)

### 9.3 Demonstratives
**Learning Value**: Pointing and specifying
- Dit / dat (het-words or standalone)
- Deze / die (de-words or standalone)
- **Usage**: Distance distinction (deze = close, die = far)

### 9.4 Reflexive & Reciprocal
**Learning Value**: Self-directed actions
- Me / je / zich / ons vergissen, schamen, herinneren
- Elkaar: "Ze zien elkaar" / "We helpen elkaar"

## 10. REGISTER & TONE MARKERS
*Formal, informal, or emotional?*

### 10.1 Formality Level
**Learning Value**: Appropriate address
- Informal (je/jij): Friend/peer conversations
- Formal (u): Professional, elderly, official settings
- **Exam Focus**: Know when to use each

### 10.2 Directness vs. Politeness
**Learning Value**: Cultural appropriateness
- Direct: "Geef me..." / "Ik wil..."
- Polite: "Zou je...?" / "Mag ik...?" / "Kun je misschien...?"
- Softened: Adding "even" / "maar" / "eens"

### 10.3 Emotional Expression
**Learning Value**: Natural reactions
- Positive: "Leuk!" / "Gelukkig!" / "Fijn!"
- Negative: "Jammer" / "Helaas" / "Vervelend"
- Surprise: "Echt?" / "Echt waar?" / "Dat wist ik niet"

## 11. SPECIAL CONSTRUCTIONS
*Distinctive Dutch patterns*

### 11.1 ER in All Its Forms
**Learning Value**: Quintessentially Dutch
- Existential: "Er is/zijn..."
- Locative: "Ik ga er naartoe"
- Quantitative: "Er zijn er drie"
- Prepositional: "Ik denk er aan" / "Ik hoor er van"
- **Learning Priority**: HIGH (very common, very confusing)

### 11.2 Pronominal Adverbs (ER + Prep)
**Learning Value**: Natural fluency
- Waar + prep (questions): "Waar denk je aan?" / "Waar kijk je naar?"
- Er + prep (statements): "Ik denk erover" / "Ik wacht erop"
- **Pattern Recognition**: Can split in sentence

### 11.3 Comparative & Superlative
**Learning Value**: Making comparisons
- Comparative: "groter dan" / "mooier dan"
- Superlative: "het grootste" / "de mooiste"
- Irregular: "beter" / "meer" / "minder"

### 11.4 Fixed Expressions & Chunks
**Learning Value**: Memorize as units
- Greetings: "Hoe gaat het?" / "Tot ziens"
- Common phrases: "Het maakt niet uit" / "Dat geeft niet"
- Time expressions: "Om de hoek" / "Over een uur"
- **Learning Priority**: HIGH (instant fluency boost)

### 12.1 Zijn, Hebben, Worden

- All tenses and forms of these three

**Learning Value**: Express nuance

**Learning Value**: Common in daily speech

- Blijven, krijgen, laten, vinden, denken
- **Pattern Recognition**: Past and participle forms

## TAGGING STRATEGY

### Primary Tag (Required)
Choose ONE from categories 1-3:
- **Communicative Intent** (what is speaker doing?)
- **Verb Pattern** (structural core)
- **Word Order Pattern** (sentence structure)

### Secondary Tags (1-3 recommended)
Add relevant tags from:
- **Time Frame** (when?)
- **Modal/Attitude** (stance?)
- **Practical Situation** (real-world context)
- **Special Construction** (distinctive features)

### Tertiary Tags (Optional)
For distinctive features:
- **Register/Tone** (if marked)
- **Pronoun Type** (if teaching point)
- **Negation** (if present)
- **Connection Type** (if complex sentence)

### Example Tagging:
**Sentence**: "Zou je morgen even naar de dokter kunnen bellen?"

**Primary**: Requesting (polite request)
**Secondary**:
- Modal verb construction (zullen + kunnen + infinitive)
- Healthcare situation
- Politeness markers (zou + even)
**Tertiary**:
- Future time frame (morgen)
- Separable verb (opbellen)

## PRIORITIZATION GUIDE FOR LEARNERS

### TIER 1: Survival Patterns (Weeks 1-4)
Learn these first for immediate communication:
- Simple present declaratives
- Yes/No questions with hebben/zijn
- Modal verb + infinitive (kunnen, mogen, moeten, willen)
- Basic wh-questions (waar, wat, wanneer, hoe)
- Polite requests (mag ik, kun je)
- Negation with niet/geen
- Present perfect with hebben (past events)

### TIER 2: Daily Fluency (Weeks 5-8)
Build on foundation:
- Subordinate clauses with omdat/als/dat
- Separable verbs (common ones)
- Gaan + infinitive (future plans)
- ER constructions (existential)
- Time connectors (eerst, dan, daarna)
- Coordination with en/maar/want
- Common modal particles (maar, even, toch)

### TIER 3: Exam Readiness (Weeks 9-12)
Refine and expand:
- Te + infinitive constructions
- Relative clauses (die/dat)
- Complex subordination
- Pronominal adverbs (ervoor, eraan, etc.)
- Past tense (simple past for common verbs)
- Advanced politeness strategies
- Situation-specific vocabulary clusters

## NOTES ON USAGE

**Keep Tags Actionable**: Every tag should answer "What can I practice with this sentence?"

**Avoid Over-Tagging**: 2-4 tags per sentence is ideal. Focus on most distinctive features.

**Think Like a Learner**: Would this distinction help someone study more efficiently?

**Exam-First When Tied**: If choosing between two tags, prefer the one more relevant to A2 exams.

**Pattern Over Grammar**: Tag "Modal + infinitive pattern" rather than "Future tense auxiliary verb construction"

**Context Matters**: Same structure can serve different intents - tag the actual communicative function in context.

This taxonomy is a living document. Adjust categories based on what patterns actually emerge from the 1,265 sentences.
