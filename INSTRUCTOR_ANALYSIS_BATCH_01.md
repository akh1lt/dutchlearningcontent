## 2. CRITICAL SENTENCE PATTERNS (Real Examples from Texts)

### PATTERN 1: "Dat lijkt me + adjective" - Expressing Opinion/Interest
**Frequency**: 2x (same speaker, different contexts)
- "Dat lijkt me heerlijk" (line 1, line 9)
- "Dat lijkt me wel leuk, maar..." (line 7)

**A2 Exam Value**: CRITICAL - This is a core pattern for expressing reactions and opinions politely.

**Teaching Priority**: MEMORIZE AS CHUNK
- Dat lijkt me leuk/goed/moeilijk/interessant
- Use for: Responding to suggestions, showing interest, polite opinions

---

### PATTERN 2: "Kun jij/je + infinitive?" - Making Requests
**Frequency**: 1x direct, multiple implied
- "Kun jij Joris vanmiddag naar het hockeyveld brengen?" (line 19)

**A2 Exam Value**: CRITICAL - Essential for asking favors, daily negotiations

**Teaching Priority**: HIGH - Combine with modal verbs (kun/kan, wil/wilt, moet)
- Kun je mij helpen?
- Kun jij dat doen?
- Kan Hans dat niet doen?

---

### PATTERN 3: "Ik moet + infinitive" - Expressing Obligation
**Frequency**: 4x
- "Ik moet Joris vanmiddag naar hockeytraining brengen" (line 7)
- "We moeten trainen" (line 22)
- "we moeten ze overal naartoe brengen" (line 31)

**A2 Exam Value**: CRITICAL - One of top 5 most-used patterns in daily Dutch

**Teaching Priority**: ESSENTIAL
- Ik moet naar de dokter
- Ik moet werken
- We moeten boodschappen doen

---

### PATTERN 4: "Ga je/gaan we + infinitive?" - Suggesting/Planning
**Frequency**: 4x
- "ga je vanmiddag mee zwemmen?" (line 6)
- "Ga je dit weekend nog wel iets leuks doen?" (line 28)
- "Laten we binnenkort samen iets gaan drinken" (line 33)
- "We gaan hardlopen" (line 22)

**A2 Exam Value**: CRITICAL - Essential for social interaction

**Teaching Priority**: MEMORIZE BOTH FORMS
- "Ga je mee?" (invitation)
- "Laten we..." (suggestion, literally "let's")

---

### PATTERN 5: "omdat + subordinate clause" - Giving Reasons
**Frequency**: 1x explicit
- "omdat we volgende week die wedstrijd hebben" (line 22)

**A2 Exam Value**: EXAM-CRITICAL - Word order in subordinate clauses is TESTED

**Teaching Priority**: PATTERN MEMORIZATION (not grammar rules)
- omdat + [rest of sentence] + VERB at end
- omdat ik ziek ben
- omdat we geen tijd hebben
- omdat dat te duur is

**Note**: This is where students struggle. Teach through repetition, not through explaining "subordinate clause word order rules."

### What Makes These Texts Excellent A2 Material

**Text 2 (Informational)**:

- Practical, useful information (holidays, school schedules)
- Date and time expressions (repeatedly tested)
- Scanning for specific information practice
- Cultural knowledge integrated (Koningsdag, WWII remembrance)

### Common A2 Traps Found in These Texts

**1. "Dat lijkt me" vs "Ik vind"**
- Students often confuse these
- "Dat lijkt me heerlijk" = That seems lovely to me (polite, softer)
- "Ik vind het heerlijk" = I find it lovely (more direct)
- Text 1 uses the softer form - this is MORE common in Dutch culture

**2. "Gaan + infinitive" for near future**
- "We gaan hardlopen" = We're going to run (near future plan)
- Students often try to use formal future tense - unnecessary at A2

**3. Word order with "omdat"**
- "omdat we volgende week die wedstrijd hebben"
- Students want to write: "omdat we hebben volgende week die wedstrijd"
- The verb MUST go to the end in subordinate clauses

**4. "Sowieso" placement**
- "Ik ga sowieso" - natural placement
- Students often put it at the end: "Ik ga, sowieso" - sounds unnatural
