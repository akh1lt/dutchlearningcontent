This hierarchical taxonomy is designed for a tagging system to categorize Dutch sentences at the A1–A2 level. It prioritizes syntactic function and morphological categories while accounting for the specific idiomatic nuances (like modal particles) common in beginner-to-intermediate Dutch.

## Dutch Grammar Taxonomy (A1–A2)

1. **Syntax & Clause Structure**
   ├── **Sentence Type**
   │   ├── Declarative
   │   ├── Interrogative (Yes/No)
   │   ├── Interrogative (Wh-question)
   │   ├── Imperative
   │   └── Exclamatory
   ├── **Word Order (Linearity)**
   │   ├── SVO (Standard)
   │   ├── V1 (Inversion)
   │   ├── V2 (Main Clause)
   │   └── SOV (Subordinate Clause)
   ├── **Clause Connectivity**
   │   ├── Main Clause (Independent)
   │   ├── Coordinate Clause (En/Maar/Of/Want)
   │   └── Subordinate Clause (Omdat/Dat/Als/Toen)
   └── **Information Structure**
       ├── Topic-Fronting
       └── Dummy-Subject (Er-Presentative)

2. **Verb System (Verbum)**
   ├── **Inflectional State**
   │   ├── Finite Verb
   │   ├── Infinitive
   │   └── Past Participle
   ├── **Tense & Aspect**
   │   ├── Presens (OTT)
   │   ├── Perfectum (VTT)
   │   ├── Imperfectum (OVTT)
   │   └── Future (Zullen / Gaan)
   ├── **Verb Clusters**
   │   ├── Auxiliary + Infinitive
   │   ├── Auxiliary + Participle
   │   ├── Modal + Infinitive
   │   └── Te-Infinitive / Om-Te-Infinitive
   └── **Morpho-Syntactic Class**
       ├── Weak Verbs
       ├── Strong Verbs
       ├── Irregular Verbs
       ├── Separable Verbs (Scheidbare werkwoorden)
       └── Reflexive Verbs

3. **Noun Phrase (NP)**
   ├── **Determinative**
   │   ├── Definite Article (De/Het)
   │   ├── Indefinite Article (Een)
   │   └── Demonstrative (Deze/Die/Dit/Dat)
   ├── **Adjectival Modification**
   │   ├── Attributive (+e inflection)
   │   ├── Predicative (Base form)
   │   └── Comparative / Superlative
   └── **Noun Properties**
       ├── Common Gender (De-woord)
       ├── Neuter Gender (Het-woord)
       ├── Singular
       ├── Plural (-en / -s)
       └── Diminutive (-tje)

4. **Pronoun System (Pronomina)**
   ├── **Personal**
   │   ├── Subject (Full/Reduced)
   │   └── Object (Full/Reduced)
   ├── **Possessive**
   │   ├── Pre-nominal (Mijn/Jouw)
   │   └── Independent
   ├── **Reflexive** (Me/Je/Zich)
   ├── **Relative** (Die/Dat/Wat)
   └── **Pronominal Adverbs**
       ├── Locative Er (Er + Preposition)
       └── Quantitative Er

5. **Particles & Modality**
   ├── **Modal Particles** (Focus: A1-A2)
   │   ├── Toch
   │   ├── Maar
   │   ├── Hoor
   │   ├── Even
   │   └── Wel
   ├── **Negation**
   │   ├── Sentential (Niet)
   │   └── Nominal (Geen)
   └── **Adverbial Modifiers**
       ├── Temporal (Nu/Straks/Gisteren)
       ├── Modal (Misschien/Zeker)
       └── Degree (Heel/Erg/Te)

6. **Prepositional & Locative Logic**
   ├── **Simple Prepositions**
   ├── **Circumpositions** (E.g., Naar ... toe)
   └── **Postpositions** (E.g., De berg op)

7. **Discourse & Logic**
   ├── **Connectors (Cohesive Devices)**
   │   ├── Additive
   │   ├── Adversative
   │   ├── Causal
   │   └── Conditional
   └── **Interjections**
       ├── Phatic (Hé, Ja, Oké)
       └── Hesitation Markers
